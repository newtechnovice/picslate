    About This Project

    There is an obvious noticeable separation of culture and modernity when it comes to topics about globalization. Preservation of our cultural heritage has clearly been disregarded by the current modern movement and is affecting all parts of our history and culture, particularly our language. However, that does not mean they have to stay at opposite ends at all times. Modernization, especially new technologies, can be utilized precisely for cultural preservation of our rich background and history.

    The primary purpose of this study is to develop a photo translation application focusing on the aforementioned indigenous languages: Cavite Chavacano, Kinaray-a, and Hiligaynon. The application can take scene images with text of these languages and translate it to the Filipino language. The secondary purpose of this study is the aim to revitalize these indigenous languages, and to help in conserving and preserving their existence. Another indirect purpose of this study is to raise awareness by learning the language and culture of a particular area. Through this, we are getting an intangible linguistic insight beyond what we normally see in mass media. This makes it easier for us to understand why we need to conserve and preserve an endangered language.

    How Does It Work?

    1. Click the "Choose File" button.
    2. Upload an image from your device gallery or take a picture if you are using a smartphone.
    3. Select the source language from the dropdown menu.
    4. Check the checkbox if the text on your uploaded image is dark (for example: black, dark blue, etc.). Otherwise, leave it unchecked.
    5. Click the "Submit" button.
